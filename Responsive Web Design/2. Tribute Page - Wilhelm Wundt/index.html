Wilhelm Wundt

Founder of psychology a.k.a the father of psychology

Wilhelm Wundt's life in perspective:

1832: Birth

  • Born on August 16, 1832, in Neckarau, Baden (modern-day Germany).

1851 - 1856: Education

  • Studied medicine at the University of Heidelberg and later in TÃ¼bingen and Berlin.

1857: Doctorate and Early Academic Career

  • Completed his doctoral studies in medicine at the University of Heidelberg.
  • Worked as an assistant to Hermann von Helmholtz, a pioneer in physiology.

1862: Began Writing on Psychology

  • Published his first work on psychology: Contributions to the Theory of Sensory Perception.

1874: Published Principles of Physiological Psychology

  • This book laid the groundwork for experimental psychology by combining physiology and psychology.

1875: Appointment at the University of Leipzig

  • Became a professor of philosophy at the University of Leipzig, where he conducted most of his groundbreaking work.

1879: Established the First Psychology Laboratory

  • Founded the first experimental psychology laboratory at the University of Leipzig, often considered the birth of modern psychology.

1881: Launched the First Psychology Journal

  • Started the journal Philosophische Studien (Philosophical Studies), devoted to psychological research.

1883 - 1903: Mentorship of Prominent Psychologists

  • Mentored many influential figures in psychology, including Edward Titchener and G. Stanley Hall, who brought his experimental methods to the U.S.

1902: Published Ethics and the Science of Psychology

  • His work increasingly explored philosophical aspects of psychology.

1917: Final Major Publication

  • Released Elements of Folk Psychology, a study on the development of culture and the collective human mind.

1920: Death

  • Died on August 31, 1920, in Grossbothen, Germany, at the age of 88.
